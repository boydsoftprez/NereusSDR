Findings:
  • NereusSDR already promoted Hardware to top-level and split off Diagnostics + CAT/Network — that's a NereusSDR-native restructure of Thetis's General mega-tab. Already done.
  • Antenna / ALEX exists as a single Hardware sub-tab — not separate "Alex Filters" top-level as I drew. My earlier "case for top-level Alex" was wrong; the tab already lives in the right place. Phase B (HPF/LPF bypass) and Phase F (antenna routing) just fill it out.
  • OC Outputs is already a Hardware sub-tab — Thetis buries it under Penny/Hermes Ctrl. NereusSDR already promoted it. Phase D fills the matrix in.
  • HL2 I/O is a placeholder Hardware sub-tab — Phase E lands the actual content (N2ADR, IoBoard version, I2C state).
  • PA Calibration exists under Hardware — Thetis has it under PA Settings. Phase G (level cal) and Phase H (PA temp/current readout) extend it. Frequency calibration could be a new "Calibration" sub-tab here, or stay under General.
  • Bandwidth Monitor already exists — Phase E (HL2 throttle hooks) plugs into it.
  • Phase C (preamp combo) isn't a Setup page — it's RxApplet UI. No Setup work.

Two real questions now:

1. Where does frequency calibration belong? Options: new Hardware → Calibration sub-tab (groups all cal); add to existing PA Calibration tab and rename it Calibration; or keep with Thetis under General → Calibration.

2. The Antenna / ALEX tab is going to get dense (per-band HPF bypass × 14 + per-band LPF bypass × 14 + per-band antenna × 14 + Alex-2 toggles). Should we split it into Antenna / ALEX → Filters / Antennas / Alex-2 sub-sub-tabs (matching Thetis's Alex-1 / Alex-2 split) or keep it flat with collapsible sections?
